Formatting Assumptions, Inputs, and Formulas for Clarity

← Back to Home

In fast-paced finance environments, clarity in your Excel models is non-negotiable. Well-formatted spreadsheets allow teammates and senior reviewers to immediately understand what’s editable, what’s calculated, and how the model flows. When everyone’s aligned visually, collaboration is smoother, feedback loops are faster, and revisions are more focused.

  • Use blue font for all manual inputs
  • Leave formulas in black font
  • Apply a light gray fill for historicals
  • Bold row and column headers
  • Use consistent number formatting
  • Label your units (e.g., $M, $K)
