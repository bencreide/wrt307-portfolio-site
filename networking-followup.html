Overview: Following Up and Staying in Touch

“A follow-up message is the bridge between a good conversation and a meaningful relationship. In fast-paced industries like finance, private equity, and consulting, people are busy — but they remember those who follow up thoughtfully. A short, well-crafted message reinforces that you were engaged, professional, and respectful of their time.”

Step 1: Follow Up Within 24 Hours

Send your follow-up promptly—ideally within a day of your conversation. Acting quickly shows that you value the person’s time and the insights they shared. It also ensures your interaction remains fresh in both your minds.

Example: “Thank you for your time yesterday; I appreciated learning about your approach to structuring LBO models.”

Step 2: Thank Them and Reference Something Specific

Open with genuine gratitude, then mention a detail or insight from your chat. This demonstrates active listening and makes your message feel personalized.

Example: “I’m grateful for your perspective on last quarter’s debt markets—it helped me refine my own analysis.”

Step 3: Mention Next Steps If Relevant

If you agreed to an action—such as sending a document or an introduction—remind them of it. This keeps both parties aligned on what comes next and maintains momentum.

Example: “As promised, I’ve attached the summary of my cap-rate analysis for your review.”

Step 4: Keep It Short and Warm

Aim for brevity: one to three concise paragraphs. A friendly, professional tone shows respect for their busy schedule while reinforcing your interest.

Example: “I know you’re busy, so I’ll keep this brief—just wanted to say how much I appreciated your guidance.”

Step 5: Set a Reminder to Reconnect

A single follow-up shouldn’t be the end. Schedule yourself a note to check in again in a few weeks—perhaps sharing an update or asking a brief question. Consistent follow-through builds lasting rapport.

Example: Calendar reminder “Check in with Jane on Q2 market trends” set for three weeks later.
