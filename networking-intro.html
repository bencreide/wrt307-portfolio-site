Overview: Creating an Effective Networking Introduction

“A strong introduction is more than just a formality — it’s a tool that sets the tone for a meaningful professional relationship. Whether it’s through email, LinkedIn, or a live event, how you introduce yourself reflects confidence, clarity, and purpose.”

Step 1: Start with Who You Are and What You’re Studying

Introduce yourself with your name, school, and field of study.

Hi, I’m Ben — I’m a junior at Syracuse University studying Finance and Real Estate.

Step 2: Mention a Relevant Experience or Career Interest

Add credibility and context by briefly describing your experience or goals.

I interned at a real estate investment firm last summer and have been focused on underwriting and acquisitions.
I’m currently preparing for private equity interviews and looking to learn more about real estate.

Step 3: Make a Quick, Specific Connection to Their Work

Show that you’ve done your research or remember a past interaction.

I’ve followed your work at Blackstone — especially your team’s approach to adaptive reuse.
I read your piece on PropTech in Bisnow — it was one of the most actionable perspectives I’ve seen.

Step 4: Close with a Light, Open-Ended Prompt or Question

End with a conversational follow-up or respectful prompt.

I’d love to hear how you got started in real estate private equity.
If you ever have a moment, I’d appreciate any advice on approaching my recruiting timeline.

Step 5 (Optional): Add a Friendly Opener or Sign-Off

Include a warmer line when appropriate to build rapport.

Hope you’re having a great start to the week!
Thanks again for your time — I know you’re busy.

Final Example Message

Hi, I’m Ben — a junior at Syracuse University studying Real Estate and Finance. Strong Intro Line

I interned at a real estate investment firm last summer and have been diving deeper into private equity. Relevant Experience

I’ve followed your work at Brookfield, especially on your recent London office-to-resi deals. Specific Connection

I’d love to hear how you found your way into the industry and what you think matters most for analysts early in their career. Clear Closing Prompt

Thanks again, and hope your week’s off to a strong start! Friendly Sign-Off
